01 — About

Background

Engineering Background

I trained as a mechanical engineer, completed a Master's in Design & Engineering at Politecnico di Milano, and went on to spend three years at ZF Group as a design engineer in the EV Powertrain division — one of the most technically demanding environments in Tier-1 automotive. My day-to-day was component drawings, GD&T callouts, tolerance stack-ups, APQP documentation, and making sure parts actually fit together the first time.

Before ZF, I worked in product design at PRM DESIGN in Italy — plastic product design, toy mechanisms, prototyping, and DFM reviews. That experience taught me to think about geometry and manufacturability together, not separately. It's a discipline that shaped how I approach every drawing I create.

I think carefully about tolerance chains, interface geometry, and drawing clarity — not just whether a part is technically correct, but whether it communicates clearly to manufacturing. That combination of design precision and documentation discipline is what I bring to mechanical design roles.
